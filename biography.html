Childhood

Vusi Nova, born Vusimuzi Nongxa on 24 September 1984 in New Brighton, Gqeberha, South Africa, was raised by his grandmother, a church choir singer. His early exposure to gospel and community choirs shaped his soulful voice and emotional style.

Image credit: Music In Africa

Early Career

At age 14, Vusi moved to Johannesburg to pursue music, studying at the National School of the Arts. He signed with EMI Records in 2009, releasing Ndimfumene, and later joined Kalawa Jazmee Records. His career truly took off after signing with Muthaland Entertainment in 2012.

Image credit: Briefly News

Breakthrough Albums

Walk Into Light (2013) introduced his Afro-soul sound with collaborations from Moneoa and RJ Benjamin. Did It For Love (2014) earned a SAMA nomination. His platinum-selling Naninina (2016) was a heartfelt tribute to his late mother and grandmother, winning multiple awards. Mayan-Nyan (2018) went gold and was nominated for Album of the Year. NguMama (2021) won Best Afro Pop Album at the SAMAs.

Image credit: SABC News

Achievements

Vusi Nova has won multiple Metro FM Awards and South African Music Awards. His hits include Thandiwe, As’phelelanga (with Jessica Mbangeni), and Yibanathi (with Dumi Mkokstad). In 2023, he launched Nova Sounds to mentor emerging artists.
